Order of a group

1 DEFINIZIONE

The order of a group is its cardinality, i.e., the number of elements in its set. Also, the order, sometimes period, of an element a of a group is the smallest positive integer m such that am = e (where e denotes the identity element of the group, and am denotes the product of m copies of a). If no such m exists, a is said to have inﬁnite order.

NB: La stessa deﬁnizione può essere data con la notazione additiva.

2 NOTAZIONE

The order of a group G is denoted by ord(G) or |G| and the order of an element a is denoted by ord(a) or |a|.
