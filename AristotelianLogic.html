1 DEFINIZIONE

Aristotelian or traditional logic are is a “subject-predicate” logic and is therefore concerned only with a portion of the sum total of logical truth. [Mil15]

It confines itself to the four forms of categorical proposition known as the A, E, I, and O forms. [Mil15]

In the second place, it treats subalternation as a valid form of inference. That is, it assigns (tacitly at least) conventional meanings, different from those employed in modern (i.e. symbolic or mathematical) logic, to the four categorical forms, meanings such that subalternation holds. [Mil15]
