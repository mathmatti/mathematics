1 DEFINITION

A derangement is a permutation of the elements of a set, such that no element appears in its original position. [?]

2 NOTATION

The number of derangement of a set of size n, usually written Dn, dn, or !n, is called the ”derangement number” or ”de Montmort number”. (These numbers are generalized to rencontres numbers). [?]

The number of derangements of an n-element set is called the nth derangement number or rencontres number, or the subfactorial of n and is sometimes denoted !n or Dn

6 HISTORY

The problem of counting derangements was first considered by Pierre Raymond de Montmort in 1708; he solved it in 1713, as did Nicholas Bernoulli at about the same time. [?]
